2. What is your one project/achievement that you are most proud of? Why?

I worked on a salon booking platform as part of the WSO2 Linux Systems Administration and DevOps Engineering Program. I was placed in a group of 7 complete strangers — all from different backgrounds, none of us with any prior experience in DevOps tools. We had to build a full microservices architecture from scratch, handling the entire pipeline ourselves using Kubernetes, Docker, Istio, Jaeger, Kiali, OpenSearch, Prometheus, Grafana, Ansible, and Terraform, all on $800 of AWS credits.

On top of that we had university exams running through the same period and a very tight deadline to deliver. I took on the Ansible automation, Istio service mesh configuration, and the observability setup with Kiali and Jaeger — all tools I was learning at the same time as building with them.

Why I'm proud of this is how we pulled it off amid so many challenges. Seven strangers from completely different backgrounds, no shared DevOps experience, real academic pressure, and with a limited time.

I wrote a full technical breakdown of my journey here:
https://medium.com/@ritharaedirisinghe/my-journey-through-the-wso2-linux-systems-administration-devops-engineering-training-f15e22af9246

3. What kind of problems or challenges motivate you the most to solve them?

Whenever I run into something that's more complicated than it needs to be, I want to fix it. Like when I was shopping for earbuds — I had to go through site after site manually comparing prices and offers for the same product. It was annoying enough that I built a webscraper that pulled all of that into one place.

That's kind of how I approach things in general. I prefer diving straight into the problem rather than reading about it first. I'd rather figure it out as I go than wait until I feel "ready."
